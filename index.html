Viktor Frankl (1905 - 1997)



Timeline of Viktor Frankl's Life:

  • 1905 - Viktor Emil Frankl is born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary
  • 1923 - Enrolls into University of Vienna to study medicine after graduating high school, specializing in neurology and psychiatry, specifically depression and suicide.
  • 1924 - First scientific paper gets published in The International Journal of Psychoanalysis. Becomes president of the Social Democratic Party of Austria's youth movement.
  • 1926 - Logotherapy is born: the theory that meaning is the central motivational force in human beings, though it is met with resistance from his mentor Alfred Adler, who shunned him for the theory.
  • 1930 - He graduates with his M.D. and starts working at Steinhof Psychiatric Hospital until he left to start his own private practice.
  • 1940 - Frankl joins the Rothschild Hospital after his private practice is impacted by the Nazi annexation of Austria, helping patients avoid the Nazi camps for the mentally disabled, until his deportation to a Nazi camp.
  • 1946 - Man's Search for Meaning is published in German, later in English, becoming a international best seller
  • 1948 - Earned PhD in philosophy from University of Vienna. Became head of neurology dept at Vienna Polyclinic Hospital & started private practice back up, working with patients till 1970
  • 1955 - Became neurology professor at University of Vienna, gave lectures as he traveled to different universities, such as Harvard.

    • Here's a list of his awards and accolades:

  • 1956 - Promotional Award for Public Education of the Ministry of Education, Austria
  • 1962 - Cardinal Innitzer Prize, Austria
  • 1969 - Austrian Cross of Honour for Science and Art, 1st class
  • 1976 - Prize of the Danubia Foundation
  • 1980 - Honorary Ring of Vienna, Austria
  • 1981 - Austrian Decoration for Science and Art
  • 1985 - Oskar Pfister Award, USA
  • 1986 - Honorary doctorate from University of Vienna, Austria
  • 1988 - Great Silver Medal with Star for Services to the Republic of Austria
  • 1995 - Hans Prinzhorn Medal
  • 1995 - Honorary Citizen of the City of Vienna
  • 1995 - Great Gold Medal with Star for Services to the Republic of Austria
  • 1997 - Viktor Frankl dies of heart failure and was buried in the Jewish part of the Vienna Central Cemetery
